# Rare Causes Reference

Conditions beyond standard IM textbook scope that cause diagnostic surprises. Consult when common DDx doesn't fit the clinical pattern.

## Coronary Artery Aneurysms (Young Patient)

| Condition | Mechanism | Key Findings | Inheritance |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| **NF1 vasculopathy** | Neurofibromin deficiency → Ras/MAPK → intimal proliferation | ≥6 CAL spots, neurofibromas, axillary freckling; non-inflammatory (normal ESR/CRP) | AD |
| **Kawasaki disease (late)** | Childhood vasculitis → persistent coronary aneurysms | 3-vessel aneurysms; thrombotic MI; may be undiagnosed in childhood | Non-hereditary |
| **Loeys-Dietz** | TGFBR1/2 mutations → arterial tortuosity + aneurysms | Bifid uvula, hypertelorism, translucent skin | AD |
| **Vascular Ehlers-Danlos (IV)** | COL3A1 → arterial rupture/dissection | Thin translucent skin, easy bruising, acrogeria | AD |
| **Behçet disease** | Vasculitis → arterial aneurysms | Oral/genital ulcers, uveitis, pathergy | HLA-B51 |
| **PAN** | Medium-vessel necrotizing vasculitis | Microaneurysms on angiography; skin, kidney, GI, neuro | HBV-associated subset |

## Recurrent MI in Young Women (<40)

| Condition | % of MI <50F | Key Distinguisher | Confirmatory Test |
|-----------|-------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **SCAD** | 25-35% | Intramural hematoma on angiography; FMD association (40-70%) | Angiography + IVUS/OCT |
| **NF1 vasculopathy** | Rare | Aneurysmal thrombosis; skin neurofibromas + CAL spots | NF1 gene testing |
| **APS** | 5-10% | Recurrent arterial/venous thrombosis; no aneurysms | Lupus anticoagulant, aCL, anti-β2GP1 |
| **FMD** | Associated w/ SCAD | String-of-beads on angiography; multi-territory | Head-to-pelvis CTA/MRA |
| **Coronary vasospasm** | Variable | At rest (not exertional); responds to NTG/CCB | Provocative testing (acetylcholine) |
| **Cocaine-induced** | Variable | Vasospasm + accelerated atherosclerosis | Urine tox screen |

## Neuromuscular Respiratory Failure

| Condition | Classic Triad/Pattern | Key Test | LR+ |
|-----------|----------------------|----------|------|
| **LEMS** | Proximal weakness + areflexia + autonomic dysfunction | Anti-P/Q VGCC Ab; CMAP facilitation >100% | Ab: ~17-20; facilitation: >50 |
| **Anti-MuSK MG** | Bulbar-predominant; poor AChE inhibitor response; muscle atrophy | Anti-MuSK antibodies | High specificity |
| **Late-onset Pompe** | Proximal weakness + respiratory failure; elevated CK (usually) | Acid alpha-glucosidase assay; GAA gene | Enzyme assay diagnostic |
| **ALS (PMA variant)** | Pure LMN weakness; no sensory loss; fasciculations; NO ptosis | EMG (active denervation + reinnervation) | Clinical + EMG |

## Coronary Disease + Skin Findings

| Skin Finding | Condition | Vascular Pathology |
|-------------|-----------|-------------------|
| Neurofibromas + CAL spots + axillary freckling | **NF1** | Coronary aneurysms, renal artery stenosis |
| Papular lesions (trunk/extremities) | **Sarcoidosis** | Coronary aneurysms, cardiomyopathy, conduction disease |
| "Plucked chicken skin" papules (neck/flexures) | **PXE** | Premature arterial calcification, GI bleeding |
| Angiokeratomas (bathing trunk distribution) | **Fabry disease** | Coronary disease, LVH, renal failure |
| Livedo reticularis + skin ulcers | **APS** | Arterial/venous thrombosis |
| Malar rash + photosensitivity | **SLE** | Coronary vasculitis, Libman-Sacks endocarditis |

## When to Trigger a Syndromic Screen

Always perform a comprehensive evaluation when:
- Vascular disease in patient <50 **without** traditional risk factors
- **Autosomal dominant** family history of premature vascular death
- **Skin findings + vascular disease** in same patient (always do complete skin exam)
- **Multi-territory** disease without atherosclerotic risk factors
- Clinical pattern that **doesn't match** common DDx for the patient's demographic
- Recurrent vascular events with **no identifiable acquired cause**

### The Screen
1. Complete skin exam (CAL spots, neurofibromas, xanthomas, skin laxity, angiokeratomas)
2. Eye exam (Lisch nodules, lens subluxation, angioid streaks)
3. Vascular exam all territories (pulse asymmetry, bruits, aortic root size)
4. Connective tissue screen (joint hypermobility, arm span:height ratio, pectus)
5. 3-generation family pedigree with inheritance pattern analysis
6. Genetic testing discussion if pattern suggests monogenic disorder
