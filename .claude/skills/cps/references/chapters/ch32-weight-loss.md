# Ch32: Unintentional Weight Loss

> Clinical data compiled from primary literature. See references.bib for sources.

## Differential Diagnosis Framework
Four pivotal steps: (1) Verify weight loss is real (25-50% have not actually lost weight). (2) Screen for malabsorption. (3) Differentiate decreased intake vs increased expenditure. (4) Comprehensive history, exam, and labs to find diagnostic clues.

## Epidemiology
- Significant = >5% body weight in 6-12 months
- 15-20% of adults >65 have unintentional weight loss; 50-60% nursing home residents
- Most common causes: cancer 19-36%, depression/alcoholism 16%, nonmalignant GI 13%, unknown 22%
- Men with unintentional weight loss: 36% mortality vs ~15% stable weight

## Key Differential Diagnoses
| Diagnosis | Prevalence | Key Features | Must-Not-Miss |
|-----------|-----------|--------------|---------------|
| Cancer (GI, lung, lymphoma) | 19-36% | Anorexia, fatigue, abnormal labs | Yes |
| Depression | ~16% | Anhedonia, depressed mood >=2 weeks | No |
| Nonmalignant GI disease | ~13% | Dysphagia, pain, diarrhea | No |
| Hyperthyroidism | Part of 7% endocrine | Weight loss with increased appetite, tremor | No |
| PUD / Gastric cancer | Common | Epigastric pain, early satiety, NSAID use | Yes |
| Dementia | Common elderly | Agitation, forgetting to eat | No |
| Poor dentition | Up to 9% in >65 | 50% edentulous by 65 | No |

## Depression Screening in Weight Loss
| Test | Sens | Spec | LR+ | LR- | Notes |
|------|------|------|-----|-----|-------|
| PHQ-2 (2 screening questions) | 96-97% | 57-67% | 2.2-2.9 | 0.04-0.07 | Negative screen makes depression very unlikely |

PHQ-2: "Over the past 2 weeks, have you felt (1) down, depressed, or hopeless? (2) little interest or pleasure in doing things?" Positive to either -> full evaluation.

## Weight Loss with Normal/Increased Appetite
Suggests increased expenditure or caloric loss:
- Hyperthyroidism (TSH LR+ >99)
- Poorly controlled diabetes mellitus
- Malabsorption (celiac, pancreatic insufficiency)
- Severe COPD
- Cancer cachexia (some patients)

## Diagnostic Algorithm
1. Verify weight loss: compare prior weights, clothing/belt size (25-50% have not actually lost weight)
2. Screen for malabsorption: diarrhea, floating/malodorous stools, stool caliber changes
3. Decreased intake vs increased expenditure: normal/increased appetite = hyperthyroidism, DM, malabsorption
4. Comprehensive evaluation: full ROS, psychosocial hx, meds, dental exam, mental status
5. Labs: CBC, hepatic/renal panel, Ca, glucose, FOBT, ESR, TSH, HIV, CXR, UA
6. Follow abnormal clues: anemia -> GI cancer; elevated ALP -> metastases; hematuria -> renal/bladder CA; elevated ESR -> myeloma, temporal arteritis
7. Update preventive screening: mammogram, colonoscopy, low-dose CT (smokers >=30 pack-years)
8. If no clues: upper endoscopy + abdominal US; then close follow-up (cancer <5% but not zero)

## Clinical Pearls
- 25-50% of patients with weight loss complaint have NOT actually lost weight
- Cancer is most common single cause but NOT the cause in most patients
- Initial evaluation battery is 93% sensitive for cancer -- a negative workup is very reassuring (LR- 0.07)
- Patient who underestimates weight loss: cancer in 52% -- more concerning than overestimation
- Depression: PHQ-2 negative = LR- 0.04-0.07, effectively rules out depression
- TSH is the single most powerful screening test (LR+ >99, LR- <0.01) -- check on every weight loss patient
- Apathetic hyperthyroidism: elderly present without typical adrenergic symptoms; OR 14.8 for apathy
- Many medical illnesses cause both weight loss AND depression (20-45% cancer patients are depressed)

## Red Flags
- Documented weight loss + negative initial evaluation: close follow-up (occult cancer possible but <5%)
- Iron deficiency anemia + weight loss: GI malignancy
- Elevated alkaline phosphatase: metastatic disease
- Markedly elevated ESR: myeloma, temporal arteritis, SBE
- Hematuria + weight loss: renal cell or bladder carcinoma
- Change in stool caliber: colorectal cancer
- Early satiety + NSAID use: PUD or gastric cancer -> endoscopy
