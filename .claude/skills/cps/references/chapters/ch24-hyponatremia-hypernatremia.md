# Ch24: Hyponatremia and Hypernatremia

> Clinical data compiled from primary literature. See references.bib for sources.

## Differential Diagnosis Framework
Four pivotal steps: (1) Search for highly diagnostic clues (glucose, K+, urine osm, serum osm), (2) Assess clinical volume status, (3) In clinically euvolemic patients check urine Na to detect subtle hypovolemia, (4) Rule out hypothyroidism and adrenal insufficiency before diagnosing SIADH.

## Key Differential Diagnoses
| Diagnosis | Prevalence | Key Features |
|-----------|-----------|--------------|
| SIADH | Most common euvolemic | Urine osm >100, urine Na >30, euvolemic |
| Cirrhosis | Common hypervolemic | Ascites 100% in hyponatremic cirrhotics |
| Heart failure | Common hypervolemic | JVD, S3, edema, dyspnea |
| Thiazide-induced | Common | Diuretic use |
| Psychogenic polydipsia | 6-20% chronic psych pts | Urine osm <100 mOsm/L |
| Exercise-associated (EAH) | 6% endurance athletes | Weight gain during event, rapid onset |
| Primary adrenal insufficiency | Rare | Hyperkalemia, hypovolemia |

## SIADH Diagnostic Criteria & LR Data
| Criterion | Value | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Effective serum osm | <275 mOsm/L | Calculated: (2 x Na) + (glucose/18) |
| Urine osm | >100, usually >300 mOsm/L | ADH concentrates urine |
| Urine Na | >30 mEq/L (87% of cases) | 13-42% may have low urine Na with low Na intake |
| Clinical euvolemia | Required | No diuretic use |
| Adrenal insufficiency must be excluded | 3-4% of suspected SIADH | Only 33-41% appropriately tested |

SIADH etiologies: neurologic 9-26%, intrathoracic 11-19%, cancer 18-25%, postoperative 7-11%, drugs 8-18%.

## Cirrhosis & Hyponatremia
| Finding | Value | LR significance |
|---------|-------|-----------------|
| Ascites present | 100% of hyponatremic cirrhotics | Absence rules out cirrhosis |
| Peripheral edema | 59% | Not sensitive enough to rule out |
| Mean urine Na | 4 mEq/L (off diuretics x5 days) | Very low from decreased effective circulating volume |
| Na <=130 in cirrhosis with ascites | 22% prevalence | Marker of severity |
| Mortality without hyponatremia | 25% inpatient / 23% 3-year outpt | -- |
| Mortality with hyponatremia | 93% inpatient / 53% 3-year outpt | -- |

## Diagnostic Algorithm
1. Step 1 - Immediate clues: glucose, K+, serum osm, urine osm (<100 = water intoxication), thiazide use, recent marathon
2. Step 2 - Volume status: hypervolemic vs euvolemic vs hypovolemic
3. Step 3 - Euvolemic patients: urine Na <20 = occult hypovolemia; >30 = true euvolemia
4. Step 4 - True euvolemia: TSH and cortisol before SIADH diagnosis
5. Do NOT check urine Na in hypervolemic patients (misleadingly low)
6. Caution with saline challenge: rapid correction risk in hypovolemia; paradoxical worsening in SIADH

## Treatment Key Points
| Scenario | Treatment | Rate Limit |
|----------|-----------|------------|
| Severe symptoms (seizures/coma) | 3% saline 100 mL bolus, repeat x2 q10min | Do not delay for labs |
| Acute hyponatremia (<48h) | Aggressive correction safe | EAH: 3% saline first-line |
| Chronic hyponatremia | Slow correction mandatory | Max 8-10 mEq/L per 24h |
| ODS risk factors | Extra caution | Hypovolemic, K+ low, liver disease, Na <=105 |

## Clinical Pearls
- Most common electrolyte abnormality in hospitalized patients
- Acute hyponatremia: seizures even at Na >120; chronic: symptoms typically at Na <125
- ODS: develops 2-6 days after overly rapid correction; MRI may be normal for 4 weeks
- EAH: weight gain >2 kg = 17% develop hyponatremia vs <2% if <2 kg gain
- Normal saline in SIADH: water retained, sodium excreted -- Na can paradoxically FALL
- Desmopressin can slow or reverse overly rapid correction
- Vaptans NOT recommended in cirrhosis (FDA warning)

## Red Flags
- Seizures/coma + hyponatremia: 3% saline immediately
- Hyperkalemia + hyponatremia: primary adrenal insufficiency
- Rapid correction of chronic hyponatremia: ODS risk (spastic quadriparesis, pseudobulbar palsy, death)
- Na <=120 + cirrhosis: 93% inpatient mortality
- Paradoxical Na drop after normal saline: SIADH -- stop saline, consult
