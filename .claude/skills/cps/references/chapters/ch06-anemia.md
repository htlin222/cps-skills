## Differential Diagnosis Framework
Pathophysiologic framework after excluding acute blood loss:
1. **Reticulocyte production index (RPI)**: pivotal first step
   - RPI <2.0 → underproduction
   - RPI >2.0 → hemolysis or adequate marrow response
2. **MCV** to organize underproduction anemias (not diagnostic):
   - Microcytic (<80): iron deficiency, thalassemia, anemia of inflammation, sideroblastic
   - Normocytic: anemia of inflammation, early iron deficiency, marrow suppression
   - Macrocytic (>100): B12 deficiency, folate deficiency, alcohol, liver disease, hypothyroidism, MDS
3. For hemolytic anemias → hereditary vs acquired; intrinsic vs extrinsic

## Key Differential Diagnoses
| Diagnosis | Prevalence | Key Features | Must-Not-Miss |
|-----------|-----------|--------------|---------------|
| Iron deficiency anemia | Most common | Microcytic, low ferritin; chronic blood loss | Occult GI malignancy |
| B12 deficiency | Common in elderly | Macrocytic; neuropsych symptoms possible without anemia | Subacute combined degeneration |
| Folate deficiency | Less common | Macrocytic; alcoholism, pregnancy | Always check B12 concurrently |
| Anemia of inflammation | Very common | Normocytic; underlying infection/inflammation/malignancy | Hb <8 suggests additional cause |
| Hemolytic anemia | Variable | High RPI, elevated LDH, low haptoglobin | TTP: thrombocytopenia + schistocytes |
| Autoimmune hemolytic anemia | — | Positive direct Coombs; spherocytes | Warm vs cold antibody distinction |

## Diagnostic Algorithm
1. Exclude **acute blood loss** (hypotension, tachycardia, hematemesis, melena)
2. Calculate **RPI**:
   - RPI = (reticulocyte % x HCT/45) / maturation factor (use 2 for HCT ~25%)
   - RPI <2 → underproduction; RPI >2 → hemolysis/acute blood loss
3. If underproduction, organize by **MCV**:
   - Microcytic → check ferritin first
   - Normocytic → check ferritin, B12, folate, TSH, creatinine
   - Macrocytic → check B12, then MMA/homocysteine if B12 100-350 pg/mL
4. If hemolysis: check LDH, haptoglobin, indirect bilirubin, peripheral smear, direct Coombs
5. For iron deficiency → **always find the source** of blood loss

## B12 Deficiency Diagnostic Algorithm
| B12 Level | Interpretation | Next Step |
|-----------|---------------|-----------|
| <100 pg/mL | Deficiency present | Determine cause |
| 100-350 pg/mL | Indeterminate | Check MMA + homocysteine |
| >350 pg/mL | Deficiency unlikely | Consider other diagnoses |
- Both MMA and homocysteine elevated → deficiency confirmed
- MMA alone elevated → deficiency confirmed
- Homocysteine alone elevated → possible deficiency
- Both normal → deficiency unlikely

## MCV and Diagnostic Accuracy
- Normal MCV in 50% of patients with abnormal B12, folate, or iron studies
- 5% of iron-deficient patients have HIGH MCV
- 12% of B12/folate-deficient patients have LOW MCV
- MCV >130: nearly all have vitamin deficiency
- MCV 115-129: 50% pretest probability of vitamin deficiency

## Clinical Pearls
- Use MCV to organize thinking, NOT to diagnose cause of anemia
- No physical sign reliably rules out anemia; overall exam sensitivity ~70%
- Ferritin is an acute phase reactant: unreliable in inflammatory states
- In CKD: absolute iron deficiency = transferrin sat <20% + ferritin <100; functional = transferrin sat <20% + ferritin >=100
- B12 deficiency can cause neuropsych symptoms without anemia (28% not anemic, 17% normal MCV)
- Always check B12 before treating folate deficiency (folate can mask B12 neuro damage)
- Iron deficiency almost always = chronic blood loss; always search for GI source in men and postmenopausal women
- All men and women >50 with iron deficiency need GI evaluation regardless of other bleeding sources

## Red Flags (Must-Not-Miss)
- Pancytopenia → bone marrow infiltration, aplastic anemia, MDS, B12 deficiency
- Schistocytes on smear → TTP/HUS, DIC, mechanical hemolysis (consider prosthetic valve)
- Iron deficiency in men or postmenopausal women → rule out GI malignancy
- B12 deficiency with neurologic symptoms → treat immediately; may be irreversible
- Hb <8 with anemia of inflammation → search for additional cause
