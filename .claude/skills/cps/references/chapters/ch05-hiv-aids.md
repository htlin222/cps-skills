# Ch5: AIDS/HIV Infection

> Clinical data compiled from primary literature. See references.bib for sources.

## Differential Diagnosis Framework
Two major clinical scenarios:
1. **HIV screening/diagnosis** in asymptomatic patients → universal screening recommended (CDC, USPSTF)
2. **Headache in HIV-positive patients** → frame by 3 pivotal points:
   - Acuity: acute (<3 days) vs subacute (days-weeks) vs chronic
   - Degree of immunosuppression (CD4TL count)
   - Focal vs nonfocal neurologic presentation

## Key Differential Diagnoses -- Headache in HIV
| Diagnosis | CD4TL Threshold | Key Features | Must-Not-Miss |
|-----------|----------------|--------------|---------------|
| Cryptococcal meningitis | <100 | Subacute headache, fever; meningismus in only 25-30% | Most common meningitis in AIDS; 6-12% mortality |
| Toxoplasmic encephalitis | <100 | Focal deficits 73-88%, ring-enhancing lesions on MRI | Most common CNS mass lesion in AIDS; 27% mortality |
| Primary CNS lymphoma | <50 | Single diffusely-enhancing lesion; mental status changes | Distinguished from toxoplasmosis by SPECT |
| PML | <100 | Focal demyelination without enhancement or mass effect | JC virus; no effective treatment |
| TB meningitis | Any CD4TL | Subacute; basilar meningitis | May occur at relatively high CD4 counts |
| Bacterial meningitis | Any CD4TL | Acute onset (<3 days), high fever, meningismus | Medical emergency |

## CD4TL Count and Opportunistic Infections
| CD4TL Count | Susceptible Infections |
|-------------|----------------------|
| <200 cells/mcL | PJP, oral candidiasis |
| <100 cells/mcL | Toxoplasmosis, Candida esophagitis, Cryptococcus, disseminated histoplasmosis |
| <50 cells/mcL | CMV retinitis, disseminated MAC |

| Serum CRAG | 24 | 0.05 | Cryptococcal meningitis | 95-100% sensitive, 96% specific |
| CSF CRAG | — | — | Cryptococcal meningitis | 91-100% sensitive, 93-98% specific |

## HIV Screening: 3-Step Sequence
1. **Step 1**: 4th-generation HIV-1/2 Ag/Ab combination immunoassay (detects p24 Ag + Ab)
2. **Step 2**: HIV-1/HIV-2 Ab differentiation immunoassay (if Step 1 positive)
3. **Step 3**: HIV-1 RNA viral load (if Step 2 indeterminate/negative)
- HIV confirmed only when 2 steps are positive
- p24 Ag appears ~14 days after infection

## Diagnostic Algorithm -- Headache in HIV
1. Assess acuity → acute: consider bacterial meningitis, HSV encephalitis
2. Assess CD4TL → if >200: DDx similar to non-HIV patients
3. If CD4TL <200: obtain neuroimaging (CT then MRI)
   - Focal mass lesion → toxoplasmosis vs PCL vs PML
   - No mass lesion → LP for cryptococcal Ag, fungal culture, VDRL, TB studies
4. Ring-enhancing lesions + positive Toxoplasma IgG → empiric treatment for TE
5. Single enhancing lesion → consider PCL; SPECT or biopsy may be needed
6. Routine CSF may be NORMAL in cryptococcal meningitis → always send specific tests

## Clinical Pearls
- Acute HIV infection presents like mononucleosis; consider when EBV VCA IgM and CMV IgM are negative
- In primary HIV, viral load is almost always >10,000/mcL; low-positive should be repeated
- Universal HIV screening is standard of care (CDC: ages 13-64 in all healthcare settings)
- Cryptococcal meningitis: routine CSF normal in 19-30% of cases; always send CRAG and fungal culture
- Meningismus is absent in most cases of cryptococcal meningitis in AIDS (only 25-30%)
- Meningismus is distinctly uncommon in toxoplasmic encephalitis
- Negative serum CRAG makes cryptococcal meningitis unlikely
- A positive PPD or IGRA never proves active TB; a negative PPD or IGRA never rules out active TB
- ART adherence of 90-95% required for viral suppression; effective ART reduces transmission by >95%

## Red Flags (Must-Not-Miss)
- Acute headache + fever + meningismus in HIV → bacterial meningitis until proven otherwise
- CD4TL <200 without PJP prophylaxis → start TMP-SMX immediately
- New focal neurologic signs in AIDS → urgent neuroimaging; toxoplasmosis most common mass lesion
- Elevated intracranial pressure in cryptococcal meningitis → associated with increased mortality; requires serial LPs
- IRIS with CNS OIs → delay ART initiation at least 4 weeks in cryptococcal meningitis
