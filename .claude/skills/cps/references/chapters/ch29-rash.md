## Differential Diagnosis Framework
First pivotal point: identify primary lesion morphology. Second: determine global reaction pattern. Third: observe distribution. Diagnosis relies heavily on pattern recognition, but systematic approach avoids premature closure.

## Lesion Morphology Definitions
- **Macule**: Flat, <1 cm; **Patch**: Flat, >1 cm
- **Papule**: Solid, elevated, <1 cm; **Plaque**: Raised plateau, variable size
- **Nodule**: Solid, 1-5 cm; **Tumor**: Solid, >5 cm
- **Vesicle**: Fluid-filled, <1 cm; **Bulla**: Fluid-filled, >1 cm
- **Pustule**: Pus-filled, any size; **Wheal**: Transient edema papule/plaque
- **Comedone**: Keratinous plug (open = black; closed = flesh-colored)

## Key Differential Diagnoses
| Diagnosis | Prevalence | Key Features | Must-Not-Miss |
|-----------|-----------|--------------|---------------|
| Acne vulgaris | Very common | Comedones + papules + pustules, face/chest/back | No |
| Herpes zoster | Common | Dermatomal grouped vesicles on erythematous base, pain precedes rash | No |
| Psoriasis | 2-3% | Well-demarcated plaques, silvery scale, extensor surfaces | No |
| Contact dermatitis | Very common | Distribution matches exposure | No |
| SJS | Rare | Mucosal involvement, targetoid, drug exposure, <10% BSA | Yes |
| TEN | Rare | >30% BSA detachment, drug-related, mortality 25-35% | Yes |
| Meningococcemia | Rare | Petechiae/purpura + fever + meningeal signs | Yes |
| Pemphigus vulgaris | Rare | Flaccid bullae, mucosal erosions, Nikolsky positive | Yes |
| Rosacea | Common | Centrofacial erythema, telangiectasias, NO comedones | No |
| Bullous pemphigoid | Uncommon | Tense bullae, elderly, pruritus, Nikolsky negative | No |

## Diagnostic Findings & Likelihood Ratios
| Finding | Sens | Spec | LR+ | LR- | Target Dx |
|---------|------|------|-----|-----|-----------|
| Comedones present | ~95% | ~99% | ~95 | 0.05 | Acne vulgaris (essentially diagnostic) |
| Dermatomal vesicles | ~95% | ~99% | ~95 | 0.05 | Herpes zoster (highly specific) |
| Nikolsky sign positive | ~80% | ~95% | ~16 | 0.21 | Pemphigus vulgaris, TEN, SSSS |
| Vesicles on nose tip (Hutchinson) | ~80% | ~99% | ~80 | 0.20 | VZV ophthalmicus -> urgent ophthalmology |
| Palpable purpura | -- | -- | -- | -- | Vasculitis (leukocytoclastic, HSP, infection) |
| Nonpalpable purpura | -- | -- | -- | -- | Thrombocytopenia, DIC, senile purpura |
| Auspitz sign (pinpoint bleeding) | ~60% | ~95% | ~12 | 0.42 | Psoriasis (after scale removal) |
| KOH positive (hyphae) | ~80% | ~99% | ~80 | 0.20 | Tinea (dermatophyte; diagnostic) |
| Tzanck smear (multinucleated giants) | ~60% | ~95% | ~12 | 0.42 | HSV or VZV (cannot distinguish) |
| VZV PCR from vesicle fluid | >95% | >99% | >95 | <0.05 | Definitive VZV diagnosis |
| Mucosal erosions + targetoid lesions | ~90% | ~99% | ~90 | 0.10 | SJS/TEN (check drug exposure) |
| Circulating IgG to BMZ antigens | 70-80% | >95% | >14 | 0.21-0.32 | Bullous pemphigoid |
| Linear IgG/C3 at DEJ (DIF) | >90% | >99% | >90 | <0.10 | Bullous pemphigoid (confirmatory) |

Note: Rash diagnosis is primarily morphology-based pattern recognition. LR values above are estimated from textbook sensitivity/specificity descriptions and clinical utility data rather than formal diagnostic accuracy studies.

## Scale Pattern Differentiation
| Scale Location | Diagnosis Suggested |
|----------------|-------------------|
| Confluent over surface | Psoriasis (silvery, adherent) |
| Trailing (central collarette) | Pityriasis rosea (herald patch -> Christmas tree pattern) |
| Leading edge (peripheral) | Tinea corporis (advancing border) |
| Greasy, yellow | Seborrheic dermatitis (scalp, nasolabial, eyebrows) |

## SJS/TEN Drug Associations (Odds Ratios)
Common culprits with highest ORs from case-control data:
- Sulfonamides, allopurinol, carbamazepine, phenytoin, phenobarbital, NSAIDs (oxicam type), nevirapine, lamotrigine
- Latency typically 1-3 weeks after drug initiation
- SJS = <10% BSA; SJS/TEN overlap = 10-30%; TEN = >30% BSA

## Diagnostic Algorithm
1. Identify primary lesion (may be obscured by excoriation, crusting, lichenification)
2. Classify reaction pattern: papulosquamous, folliculopapular, blistering, purpuric, dermal
3. Assess distribution: symmetric vs asymmetric, dermatomal, sun-exposed, extensor vs flexor
4. Key history: drug exposure, timing, systemic symptoms, immunosuppression, contacts
5. Confirmatory tests: KOH (tinea), Tzanck/PCR (VZV/HSV), biopsy + DIF (autoimmune blistering)

## Clinical Pearls
- Comedones distinguish acne from rosacea (rosacea has NO comedones)
- Rosacea peaks in middle age; ocular involvement in >50%
- Psoriasis: Auspitz sign, nail pitting, Koebner phenomenon, family history
- Guttate psoriasis: often preceded by streptococcal pharyngitis
- Pityriasis rosea: herald patch then "fir tree" pattern, self-limited 8-12 weeks
- Contact dermatitis distribution matches contactant (poison ivy = linear streaks)

## Red Flags
- Palpable purpura + fever: meningococcemia, RMSF, endocarditis -- evaluate immediately
- Mucosal erosions + targetoid + drug exposure: SJS/TEN
- Rapid skin detachment >30% BSA: TEN -- ICU/burn unit, mortality 25-35%
- Vesicles on nose tip: VZV ophthalmicus -- urgent ophthalmology
- Flaccid bullae + oral erosions + Nikolsky positive: pemphigus vulgaris
- Erythroderma (>90% BSA): dermatologic emergency
