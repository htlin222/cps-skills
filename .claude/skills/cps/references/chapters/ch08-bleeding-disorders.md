# Ch8: Bleeding Disorders

> Clinical data compiled from primary literature. See references.bib for sources.

## Differential Diagnosis Framework
Distinguish by mechanism using pivotal points:
1. **Structural** vs **platelet** vs **clotting factor** causes
2. **Platelet-type bleeding**: petechiae, mucosal bleeding, immediate onset, small volume
3. **Clotting factor bleeding**: delayed, joints/deep tissue/GI, quantitatively significant
4. For thrombocytopenia: **decreased production** vs **increased destruction/consumption**
5. For coagulopathy: interpret **PT/INR** (extrinsic: factor VII) vs **aPTT** (intrinsic: XII, XI, IX, VIII)
6. **Mixing study**: corrects = factor deficiency; does not correct = inhibitor

## Key Differential Diagnoses
| Diagnosis | Prevalence | Key Features | Must-Not-Miss |
|-----------|-----------|--------------|---------------|
| ITP | ~100/million | Young women, isolated thrombocytopenia, large platelets | Clinical diagnosis; bone marrow not required |
| TTP | Rare | Pentad: thrombocytopenia, MAHA, neuro, fever, AKI | 90% fatal untreated; treat when suspected |
| HIT | 5% of heparin recipients | Platelet drop >50% on days 5-10; thrombosis in 50% | Any heparin exposure can cause; stop heparin immediately |
| Liver disease coagulopathy | Common | PT/INR prolonged > aPTT; all factors low except VIII | Factor VIII normal/elevated (distinguishes from DIC) |
| Vitamin K deficiency | Common in hospitalized | PT/INR prolonged > aPTT; poor intake or antibiotics | Takes 18-24h to correct with vitamin K |
| DIC | Varies by trigger | Acute diffuse bleeding, thrombocytopenia, prolonged PT + aPTT, low fibrinogen, high D-dimer | Treat underlying cause; replete factors |
| von Willebrand disease | 1% of population | Lifelong mucosal bleeding, menorrhagia | Most common inherited bleeding disorder |
| Hemophilia A | 1:5000 males | Joint/deep tissue bleeding, prolonged aPTT, normal PT | X-linked; factor VIII deficiency |

## PLASMIC Score for TTP
| Criterion | Points |
|-----------|--------|
| Platelet count <30,000/mcL | 1 |
| Hemolysis (elevated LDH, low haptoglobin, schistocytes) | 1 |
| Absence of active cancer | 1 |
| Absence of solid organ or stem cell transplant | 1 |
| MCV <90 fL | 1 |
| INR <1.5 | 1 |
| Creatinine <2.0 mg/dL | 1 |
- Score 6-7: high likelihood ADAMTS13 <10%

## 4T Score for HIT
| Category | 2 Points | 1 Point | 0 Points |
|----------|----------|---------|----------|
| Thrombocytopenia | Drop >50%, nadir >20K | Drop 30-50% or nadir 10-19K | Drop <30% or nadir <10K |
| Timing | Days 5-10, or <1d with recent exposure (<30d) | Consistent but data missing, or >10d | <4d without recent exposure |
| Thrombosis | New thrombosis, skin necrosis, or systemic reaction | Progressive/recurrent or suspected | None |
| Other causes | None apparent | Possible | Definite |

## Coagulation Test Patterns
| Pattern | Differential Diagnosis |
|---------|----------------------|
| Prolonged PT only | Factor VII deficiency, early vitamin K deficiency, early warfarin |
| Prolonged aPTT only | Factors VIII, IX, XI, XII deficiency; hemophilia A/B; heparin; lupus anticoagulant |
| Both PT + aPTT prolonged | Common pathway (V, X, II, fibrinogen); liver disease; DIC; vitamin K deficiency (advanced) |
| Thrombocytopenia + both prolonged | DIC; severe liver disease |

## Diagnostic Algorithm
1. Characterize the bleeding: platelet-type vs clotting factor-type
2. If platelet-type → check platelet count
   - Low platelets → decreased production vs increased destruction
   - Normal platelets → platelet dysfunction (PFA-100 assay)
3. If clotting factor-type → check PT/INR and aPTT
   - Isolated prolonged PT → factor VII (vitamin K deficiency, early liver disease, warfarin)
   - Isolated prolonged aPTT → factors VIII, IX, XI, XII (hemophilia, heparin)
   - Both prolonged → common pathway, DIC, severe liver disease
4. Mixing study to distinguish deficiency from inhibitor
5. For hospitalized patient with new thrombocytopenia → review medications (heparin!), check for sepsis

## DIC Diagnostic Workup
- Platelet count: low
- PT/INR and aPTT: prolonged
- Fibrinogen: low (<100 mg/dL correlates with bleeding risk)
- D-dimer and FDP: elevated
- Factor VIII: low (distinguishes from liver disease where VIII is normal/elevated)

## Clinical Pearls
- ITP is a clinical diagnosis; bone marrow biopsy only if atypical features (splenomegaly, other cytopenias, older age)
- TTP: thrombocytopenia + MAHA must both be present; only 2-3 of 5 classic features may be present
- HIT: any heparin exposure (including flushes) can cause; platelet count usually stays >50,000
- HIT: paradoxically causes thrombosis, not bleeding; 50% develop thrombosis
- Liver disease: factor VIII is normal or elevated; in DIC, factor VIII is low
- PT/INR disproportionately prolonged vs aPTT in both liver disease and vitamin K deficiency (factor VII has shortest half-life)
- Low factor VII + normal factor V → suggests vitamin K deficiency over liver disease
- Von Willebrand disease: most common inherited bleeding disorder; type 1 most common (80%)
- Goal in ITP treatment: safe platelet count (>30,000), not normal count

## Red Flags (Must-Not-Miss)
- Thrombocytopenia + schistocytes → TTP until proven otherwise; start plasma exchange immediately
- New thrombocytopenia on heparin (days 5-10) → stop ALL heparin immediately; start alternative anticoagulant
- Acute diffuse bleeding from multiple sites in critically ill patient → DIC; treat underlying cause
- Headache + thrombocytopenia → rule out TTP and intracranial hemorrhage
- HIT with thrombosis → requires alternative anticoagulation for 2-3 months; do NOT use warfarin until platelets recover
- Prior TTP survival improved from 10% to 90% with plasma exchange
