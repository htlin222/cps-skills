## Differential Diagnosis Framework
Anatomic-pathophysiologic framework: prerenal (~25%), intrarenal (~50%, mostly ATN), postrenal (~10%). Always evaluate for hypovolemia and obstruction first (treatable, must-not-miss). Use urine electrolytes, urinalysis with microscopy, and renal ultrasound as pivotal diagnostic tools.

## Key Differential Diagnoses
| Diagnosis | Prevalence | Key Features | Must-Not-Miss |
|-----------|-----------|--------------|---------------|
| ATN (ischemic) | ~50% of AKI | Prolonged hypotension, granular casts, FENa >1% | No |
| Prerenal azotemia | ~25% | Hypovolemia, low FENa <1%, responds to fluids | Yes (treatable) |
| Obstruction (postrenal) | ~10% (17% outpatient) | BPH, malignancy, hydronephrosis on US | Yes (treatable) |
| Contrast-induced AKI | Common post-procedure | Cr peaks day 3, returns baseline by day 10 | No |
| Acute interstitial nephritis | Uncommon | Drug-related, eosinophilia, WBC casts, rash, fever | No |
| Glomerulonephritis | Uncommon | RBC casts, hematuria, proteinuria | Yes |
| Rhabdomyolysis | Uncommon | Myoglobin, elevated CK, pigmented casts | Yes |
| Thrombotic microangiopathy | Rare | HUS/TTP, MAHA, thrombocytopenia | Yes |

## Measuring Kidney Function
- Cr of 1.0 to 1.5 mg/dL = GFR drops from 80 to 50 mL/min (50-year-old white man)
- Cr of 4.0 to 6.0 = GFR drops from 16 to 10 (much smaller absolute change)
- Trimethoprim, cimetidine, pyrimethamine: inhibit tubular Cr secretion (pseudo-AKI, no true GFR change)
- Creatinine clearance overestimates GFR (tubular secretion); urea clearance underestimates (reabsorption)

## Diagnostic Algorithm
1. **Initial workup for ALL AKI**: Urine electrolytes (Na, Cr, urea), urinalysis with microscopy, renal US
2. **Exclude obstruction**: Renal US for hydronephrosis; bladder scan for postvoid residual
   - Obstruction must be bilateral to cause AKI (unless single kidney)
   - Patients who are not anuric can still have obstruction
3. **Prerenal vs ATN**:
   - FENa: <1% prerenal, >1% ATN (with caveats)
   - FEurea: <35% prerenal, >50% ATN (better if on diuretics)
   - Urine microscopy: bland = prerenal; granular/epithelial casts = ATN
4. **Volume challenge**: If prerenal suspected, IV fluids; Cr should improve within hours
5. **If neither prerenal nor postrenal**: Evaluate intrarenal causes
6. **Urine sediment guides**:
   - RBC casts = GN (urgent workup: ANCA, anti-GBM, complement, ANA)
   - WBC casts = AIN or pyelonephritis
   - Granular/epithelial casts = ATN
   - Pigmented casts = rhabdomyolysis or hemolysis
7. **Check medications**: NSAIDs, ACEi/ARBs, aminoglycosides, contrast, cisplatin

## Contrast-Induced AKI
- Defined: Cr rise >=0.5 mg/dL or 25% increase at 48h post-procedure
- Risk factors: CKD, diabetes, dehydration, HF, anemia, hypotension, contrast volume
- Prevention: IV hydration (1 mL/kg/h x 12h pre/post); avoid NSAIDs; hold metformin day of procedure
- No benefit from N-acetylcysteine or sodium bicarbonate

## BPH and Urinary Retention
- BPH present in 80% of men in their 70s
- Acute urinary retention: 10% of men in 70s, 33% in 80s
- Prostate size does NOT correlate with symptom severity
- Digital rectal exam underestimates prostate size by 25-55%
- IPSS: 0-7 mild, 8-19 moderate, 20-35 severe

## Clinical Pearls
- Small rise in Cr (1.0 to 1.5) can reflect large drop in GFR (80 to 50)
- Combination of confusion, nonfluent speech, dry mucous membranes, dry/furrowed tongue, extremity weakness, sunken eyes is highly predictive of hypovolemia
- Patients can be hypovolemic despite normal-appearing exam findings
- ATN mortality: 15-30% hospitalized, 40-60% ICU
- 60% of ATN survivors recover over 1-2 weeks; post-ATN diuresis may occur
- CKD more likely after ATN, especially with preexisting CKD
- MAP target >=65 in sepsis; elderly may need >80-90

## Red Flags (Must-Not-Miss)
- Anuria (<100 mL/day): complete obstruction, severe ATN, vascular catastrophe, or severe GN
- Hyperkalemia, volume overload, refractory acidosis, uremic pericarditis: emergent dialysis
- RBC casts: glomerulonephritis -- urgent workup
- Rapidly rising Cr despite fluids: ATN, obstruction, or intrarenal disease
- AKI + eosinophilia + rash + fever: acute interstitial nephritis -- stop offending drug
- AKI + MAHA + thrombocytopenia: TTP/HUS -- urgent hematology
