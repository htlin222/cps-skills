## Differential Diagnosis Framework
Classification of diabetes types, screening, diagnosis, complication monitoring, and treatment.
Primary clinical tasks: who to test, who has diabetes, which complications to monitor, how to treat.

Types: Type 1 DM (5-10%; autoimmune beta cell destruction) > Type 2 DM (90-95%; insulin resistance + impaired secretion) > Other (monogenic, exocrine pancreatic, endocrinopathies, drug-induced) > Gestational DM
Note: 7.5-10% of adults with "type 2" actually have type 1 (LADA) with circulating antibodies.

## Key Differential Diagnoses
| Diagnosis | Prevalence | Key Features | Must-Not-Miss |
|-----------|-----------|--------------|---------------|
| Type 2 DM | 90-95% of DM; 9.4% US prevalence | Insulin resistance + impaired secretion; obesity key RF (RR 42-93 if BMI >35) | DKA can occur (1/3 of all DKA cases are type 2) |
| Type 1 DM | 5-10% of DM | Autoimmune; antibodies in 85-90%; always needs insulin | High DKA risk; complete insulin deficiency |
| Prediabetes (IFG/IGT) | Very common | IFG 100-125, IGT, or HbA1c 5.7-6.4% | 5-15% annual progression to DM; 10-20x higher risk |
| HHS | Type 2 DM, elderly | Glucose >600, osmolality >320, minimal ketosis | Mortality 5-6% (vs <2% DKA); 20-25% present with coma |
| LADA | 7.5-10% of "type 2" | Adults with type 1 antibodies misclassified as type 2 | Progressive insulin requirement; higher DKA risk |
| Drug-induced DM | Variable | Corticosteroids, antipsychotics, thiazides, HIV meds | Reversible if offending agent stopped |

## Screening Criteria (ADA)
- Age >=45: screen all patients
- Any age with BMI >=25 (>=23 in Asian Americans) + >=1 risk factor
- Risk factors: family history, physical inactivity, high-risk ethnicity, GDM, HTN, HDL <35, TG >250, PCOS, CVD, prediabetes
- Frequency: every 3 years if normal; every year if prediabetes

## Diagnostic Algorithm
1. Screen: age >=45 OR BMI >=25 (>=23 Asians) with >=1 risk factor
2. Diagnose: FPG >=126, HbA1c >=6.5%, or random glucose >=200 + symptoms (confirm with repeat)
3. One-third detected by FPG only, one-third by HbA1c only, one-third by both
4. Classify: type 1 vs type 2 (antibodies if uncertain); consider LADA in adults
5. Assess complications at diagnosis (type 2): retinopathy, neuropathy, nephropathy, foot exam
6. Assess cardiovascular risk: lipids, BP, smoking, obesity, ASCVD risk score
7. Monitor: HbA1c q3-6 months; annual eye exam, foot exam, albumin/creatinine ratio, creatinine

## HHS vs DKA Comparison
| Feature | DKA | HHS |
|---------|-----|-----|
| Glucose | >250 mg/dL | >600 mg/dL |
| pH | <=7.3 | >7.3 |
| HCO3 | <=18 | >18 |
| Ketones | Positive | Minimal/absent |
| Osmolality | Variable | >320 mOsm/L |
| Mortality | <2% (overall) | 5-6% |
| Mental status | Variable | Coma in 20-25%; correlates with osmolality |
| Fluid deficit | 3-6 L | ~9 L (20-25% TBW) |

## Diabetic Foot Infection Classification
| Severity | Criteria | Key Points |
|----------|----------|-----------|
| Mild | >=2 local signs, erythema <=2 cm, skin/subQ only, no SIRS | Acute usually monomicrobial |
| Moderate | Erythema >2 cm or deep structure involved, no SIRS | Osteomyelitis in 50-60% |
| Severe | Local infection + >=2 SIRS criteria | IV broad spectrum including anaerobes |

## Clinical Pearls
- 24% of people with diabetes are undiagnosed
- HbA1c falsely low: hemoglobinopathies, hemolysis, EPO use, CKD stage 4-5
- HbA1c falsely high: iron, B12, or folate deficiency
- African Americans: HbA1c 0.2-0.4% higher at same glucose levels
- DKA occurs in type 2 DM (1/3 of all DKA cases); do not assume type 1
- 50% of patients with DPN are asymptomatic; annual foot exam essential
- Osteomyelitis develops in 20% of mild and 50-60% of moderate-severe foot infections
- Normal CBC/CRP/ESR does NOT rule out osteomyelitis
- Lifetime foot ulcer risk: 19-34%; neuropathy present in nearly all ulcer patients
- Metformin: preferred initial agent; reduces micro/macrovascular outcomes + mortality (UKPDS)
- 75% of type 2 DM patients require >1 drug by 9 years
- Intensive glycemic control may harm older patients with CVD; individualize targets
- Only 14.3% of diabetic patients simultaneously meet HbA1c, BP, and cholesterol goals
- HHS: search for another cause of altered mental status if osmolality <320 mOsm/L
- Nephropathy: 20-40% of DM patients; most common cause of ESRD in US

## Red Flags (Must-Not-Miss)
- DKA in type 2 DM: 1/3 of DKA cases; do not assume all DKA is type 1
- SGLT2 inhibitor euglycemic DKA: near-normal glucose masks ketoacidosis
- HHS with osmolality >320: 50% have coma; osmolality <320 with AMS -> seek other cause
- Diabetic foot ulcer + probe-to-bone: high probability of osteomyelitis
- Charcot joints: 10% of DPN patients, tarsometatarsal region
- Cardiovascular autonomic neuropathy: silent ischemia, cardiac death risk
- Nephropathy: microalbuminuria -> macroalbuminuria -> ESRD; ACE-I/ARB indicated (not for primary prevention with normal BP/albumin/GFR)
- Retinopathy: most patients asymptomatic until advanced; annual dilated eye exam required
- Always search for precipitant in DKA/HHS: infection (UTI, pneumonia), MI, CVA, medication noncompliance
