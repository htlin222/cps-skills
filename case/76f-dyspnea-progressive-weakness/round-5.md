# Round 5: Diagnostic Conference (Consensus)

## Case Synopsis
76F, heavy smoker, presenting with neuromuscular respiratory failure requiring intubation, preceded by 6 months of progressive proximal weakness, bilateral ptosis, absent reflexes, intact sensation, normal CK, low/absent CMAPs, prior SIADH, and failure to respond to MG-directed therapy.

## Consensus: Lambert-Eaton Myasthenic Syndrome (LEMS), Paraneoplastic

### Evidence Integration Across All Rounds

**Round 1 (Attending Physician)**:
- Problem representation identified neuromuscular localization (not primary cardiac/pulmonary)
- LEMS identified as leading hypothesis at 35% based on the classic triad: proximal weakness + areflexia + autonomic dysfunction (SIADH)
- Key distinguisher from MG: areflexia + failed standard therapy

**Round 2 (Radiology & Pathology)**:
- CXR small pleural effusion cannot explain SpO2 80% → hypoxemia is hypoventilatory
- Normal CK effectively eliminates inflammatory myopathy (LR- 0.1)
- Normal CSF protein argues strongly against CIDP (LR- 0.3)
- Low/absent CMAPs = hallmark of presynaptic NMJ failure (LR+ 8.0 for LEMS)
- Probability updated to 65%

**Round 3 (Neurology)**:
- Localization confirmed as presynaptic NMJ (areflexia + low CMAPs + poor AChE response)
- RNS was inconclusive because only low-frequency stimulation was performed; high-frequency RNS needed for >100% facilitation (sensitivity 84-96%, specificity ~100%)
- Systematic exclusion: MG (areflexia + low CMAPs), ALS (ptosis + SIADH), CIDP (normal CSF), Pompe (normal CK + ptosis)
- Probability updated to 88%

**Round 4 (EBM Specialist)**:
- Anti-P/Q VGCC antibodies: sensitivity 85-95%, specificity >99% when combined with clinical criteria
- CMAP facilitation >100%: sensitivity 84-96%, specificity ~100% — pathognomonic
- DELTA-P score predicts SCLC in LEMS (AUC 94.4-94.6%): this patient likely scores ≥3 (smoker, age >50, weight loss/appetite decline)
- SIADH occurs in 10-45% of SCLC via ectopic ADH → the prior SIADH was likely paraneoplastic, NOT SSRI-related
- LEMS-SCLC patients have paradoxically better survival (17 mo vs 7 mo) due to earlier cancer detection
- 3,4-DAP (amifampridine) has Level I evidence (Cochrane meta-analysis of 7 RCTs)

## Final Probability Assessment

| Diagnosis | Final Probability | Confidence | Status |
|-----------|-------------------|------------|--------|
| **LEMS (paraneoplastic/SCLC)** | **>99%** | Very High | Leading diagnosis |
| Paraneoplastic MND | <5% | Low | Alternative if VGCC negative |
| ALS (PMA variant) | <2% | Very Low | Excluded by ptosis + SIADH |
| Seronegative MG | <1% | Very Low | Excluded by areflexia + low CMAPs |
| All others | <1% | Very Low | Excluded |

## Key Diagnostic Chain (Why LEMS and Why Not Others)

### Why LEMS:
1. **Triad complete**: Proximal weakness + areflexia + autonomic dysfunction (SIADH)
2. **Electrodiagnostic hallmark**: Low/absent CMAPs (presynaptic failure)
3. **Failed MG therapy**: Presynaptic disorder doesn't respond to postsynaptic AChE inhibitors alone
4. **Heavy smoker with SIADH**: Dual paraneoplastic markers pointing to SCLC
5. **Mild edrophonium response**: Partial, consistent with LEMS (vs strong in MG)

### Why NOT MG:
- Reflexes absent (preserved in >95% of MG patients)
- Low CMAPs (normal in MG; postsynaptic defect doesn't reduce baseline CMAP)
- Failed pyridostigmine + IVIG (effective in ~70-80% of MG)

### Why NOT ALS:
- Ptosis (very rare in ALS, even PMA variant)
- SIADH (not associated with ALS)
- Pattern doesn't match: ALS has asymmetric onset, fasciculations, UMN signs (classic) or pure LMN (PMA)

### Why NOT CIDP:
- Normal CSF protein (elevated in ~80% of CIDP)
- Respiratory failure is rare in CIDP
- Ptosis unusual for CIDP

### Why NOT Pompe:
- Normal CK (usually mildly elevated in Pompe)
- Ptosis not a feature of acid maltase deficiency
- Areflexia with normal sensation doesn't fit metabolic myopathy pattern

## Confirmatory Workup Ordered
1. **Anti-P/Q-type VGCC antibodies** — expect positive (85-90% sensitivity)
2. **Repeat RNS with high-frequency protocol (50 Hz)** or post-exercise CMAP — expect >100% facilitation
3. **CT chest with IV contrast** — expect central/hilar SCLC mass
4. **Anti-SOX1 antibodies** — 64% sensitivity for SCLC-LEMS, 95% specificity
5. **Anti-Hu antibodies** — additional paraneoplastic marker
6. **DELTA-P score calculation** — predict SCLC risk

## Treatment Plan (Pending Confirmation)
1. **Immediate**: Start amifampridine (3,4-DAP) 15-20 mg TID (Level I evidence)
2. **Plasma exchange** or IVIG (may need different dosing than MG protocol)
3. **If SCLC confirmed**: Cisplatin + etoposide chemotherapy ± thoracic radiation
4. **Long-term**: Immunosuppression (azathioprine/rituximab) if autoimmune LEMS
5. **Continue ventilatory support** until neuromuscular function improves

## Conference Summary
All four specialty perspectives converge on **Lambert-Eaton Myasthenic Syndrome** as the diagnosis with >99% post-test probability. The case beautifully illustrates how paraneoplastic syndromes can precede cancer detection — the SIADH 3 months ago was likely the first paraneoplastic manifestation of occult SCLC, followed by progressive LEMS. The key clinical pearl is that **areflexia in a patient with ptosis and weakness should immediately shift thinking from MG to LEMS**, as this single finding carries enormous discriminatory power (LR+ 8.0 for LEMS, LR 0.2 for MG).
