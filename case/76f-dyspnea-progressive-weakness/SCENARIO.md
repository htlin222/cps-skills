# 76F with Dyspnea and Progressive Muscle Weakness

## Chief Complaint
Dyspnea with oxygen saturation 80% on ambient air

## History of Present Illness
76-year-old woman presenting with 2 weeks of shortness of breath without fever, chills, or cough. Felt weak and tired with decreased appetite. Referred to ED after SpO2 80% on room air by general practitioner.

Preceding the respiratory distress: progressive muscle weakness over 6 months. MRI lumbar spine showed only mild lumbar stenosis without nerve/spinal cord impingement. Orthopedic evaluation unrevealing. Physiotherapy had little effect.

3 months prior: hospitalized for symptomatic hyponatremia → confirmed SIADH, attributed to SSRI for anxiety. Managed with fluid restriction and SSRI discontinuation.

At transfer hospital: treated with glucocorticoids, pyridostigmine, and IVIG for suspected myasthenic crisis → no notable clinical improvement. Hypoxemia worsened → intubated → transferred to neurology ICU.

## Past Medical History
- Heavy smoking history
- Dyslipidemia
- Hypertension
- Diabetes mellitus
- SIADH (3 months ago, attributed to SSRI)
- Anxiety (was on SSRI, now discontinued)

## Medications (prior to admission)
- Thiazide diuretic
- Sitagliptin
- Metformin
(NOT on glucocorticoids, statins, or immune checkpoint inhibitors)

## Vital Signs
- RR: 22 breaths/min
- SpO2: 85% on nonrebreather face mask
- BP: within normal range

## Physical Examination
### Neurologic Examination
- Mild bilateral ptosis, without diplopia or ophthalmoplegia
- Decreased strength in orbicularis oculi bilaterally
- Weak neck flexors and extensors
- Proximal muscle weakness in arms with milder distal weakness
- Severe proximal AND distal muscle weakness in legs
- Sensation to light touch: INTACT
- Reflexes: ABSENT throughout
- Severe leg edema (from prior hospitalization, attributed to fluid overload)

## Laboratory Data
- Routine labs: unremarkable
- Creatine kinase: normal
- CSF: no cells, normal protein and glucose, cytology negative for cancer

## Imaging
- CXR: small pleural effusion
- MRI lumbar spine (prior): mild lumbar stenosis only

## Additional Studies
- Nerve conduction study: low-amplitude or absent compound muscle action potentials (CMAPs) in arms and legs
- Repetitive nerve stimulation: inconclusive
- Edrophonium test: mild reduction in eye symptoms
- EMG: not performed (severe leg edema)
- IVIG trial: no improvement
- Pyridostigmine trial: no improvement

## Clinical Course
Patient intubated, in neurology ICU, not sedated. Cannot speak (intubated) or write (weakness). Progressive deterioration despite MG-directed therapy.
