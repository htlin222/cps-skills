## Radiologist Assessment

### CXR: Cardiomegaly
- **Enlarged cardiac silhouette** without pulmonary edema = chronic ventricular remodeling/dilation
- Consistent with **prior MI with LV dilation** (post-infarction cardiomyopathy from her previous inferior MI)
- Cardiomegaly on CXR: sensitivity 74%, specificity 78% for HF → LR+ 3.4 when present
- **No widened mediastinum** → argues against aortic dissection (LR- 0.08 for dissection when no mediastinal widening)
- No infiltrates → pneumonia excluded
- No pneumothorax → excluded

### ECG Interpretation (Critical)
This ECG tells two stories simultaneously:

**Story 1 — Old inferior MI (Q waves II/III/aVF)**:
- Confirms the prior MI was **inferior territory** (RCA or LCx distribution)
- Q waves = transmural infarction with permanent myocardial loss
- RBBB may be chronic from prior damage or new from current event

**Story 2 — Acute anterior STEMI (ST elevation V2-V5)**:
- ST elevation in V2-V5 = **LAD territory** acute infarction
- This is a DIFFERENT territory from the prior MI (inferior → now anterior)
- LR+ for acute MI with ST elevation: **5.7-53** (from Ch9 reference)

**Multi-territory implication**: Two separate coronary events in different territories (inferior + anterior) in a 38-year-old woman. This is the most important finding for narrowing the differential.

## Pathologist Assessment

### Troponin I: 0.2 ng/mL (5× ULN)
- Confirms **acute myocardial injury/necrosis**
- Troponin LR+ 48, LR- 0.05 → diagnosis of acute MI is now certain
- Initial troponin may still be rising — serial troponins needed

### Complete Blood Count: **Microcytic Anemia**
This is a critical unexpected finding:
- **Hgb 9.0** = moderate anemia
- **MCV 65 fL** = microcytic (normal 80-100)
- **RDW 18%** = elevated (normal <14.5%) → anisocytosis
- **Platelets 355k** = reactive thrombocytosis

The pattern (low MCV + high RDW + reactive thrombocytosis) is classic for **iron deficiency anemia (IDA)**.

In a 38-year-old premenopausal woman, the most common cause is **menorrhagia**. However, this must also raise concern for:
- **GI blood loss** (ulcer, malignancy — less likely at age 38)
- **Chronic disease** (autoimmune, inflammatory)
- **Thalassemia trait** (but RDW >18% favors IDA over thalassemia, which has normal RDW)

**Clinical significance for the cardiac presentation**:
- Anemia reduces oxygen delivery → can **precipitate or worsen myocardial ischemia**
- Hgb 9.0 with acute MI = increased mortality risk
- The physical exertion (mail carrier) + anemia = demand-supply mismatch on top of coronary disease

### WBC: Leukocytosis with Neutrophilia
- WBC 11.7k with 87% neutrophils = stress leukocytosis
- Expected in acute MI (catecholamine-driven demargination)
- Does not suggest infection (no fever, normal lungs)

### CMP Normal
- Rules out metabolic causes (hypokalemia, hypocalcemia, renal failure)
- Normal glucose → no acute DKA/HHS

### Critical New Consideration: Antiphospholipid Syndrome
APS should be strongly considered because:
1. **Recurrent arterial thrombosis** in a young woman — hallmark of APS
2. **Multi-territory coronary events** (inferior + anterior) — APS causes thrombosis in any vascular bed
3. **Microcytic anemia** — APS is associated with autoimmune hemolytic anemia (AIHA), though this appears iron-deficient rather than hemolytic; however, APS can occur with SLE which causes anemia of chronic disease
4. **Young woman** — APS predominantly affects women of childbearing age
5. **No traditional atherosclerotic risk factors** visible

## Recommendations

### Immediate (Acute STEMI Protocol)
1. **Emergent cardiac catheterization** — STEMI with ST elevation V2-V5 = cath lab activation
2. **At cath**: Carefully assess coronary anatomy for SCAD (intramural hematoma, dissection flap) vs atherosclerotic plaque vs thrombosis without plaque (APS)
3. **IVUS/OCT** if coronary findings are ambiguous
4. **Transfuse if Hgb drops further** — target Hgb >8 in acute MI setting

### Post-Cath Workup
5. **Antiphospholipid antibodies**: Lupus anticoagulant, anticardiolipin IgG/IgM, anti-β2-glycoprotein I
6. **ANA, anti-dsDNA, complement levels** (screen for SLE)
7. **Iron studies**: Ferritin, TIBC, serum iron (confirm IDA)
8. **Reticulocyte count + peripheral smear** (rule out hemolysis)
9. **Lipid panel** (assess for occult dyslipidemia/FH despite no xanthomas)
10. **Retrieve prior MI records** — was the inferior MI diagnosed as SCAD or atherosclerotic?
11. **Echocardiogram** — assess LV function, wall motion abnormalities, intracardiac thrombus
12. **Head-to-pelvis CTA/MRA** if SCAD confirmed (FMD screening)

## Summary
The ECG confirms **acute anterior STEMI** (new) superimposed on **old inferior MI** (Q waves). Multi-territory coronary disease in a 38-year-old woman without traditional risk factors raises the differential beyond SCAD to include **antiphospholipid syndrome** and **coronary vasculitis**. The **microcytic anemia** (Hgb 9.0, MCV 65) is an important comorbidity that likely worsened ischemia and requires evaluation. The cath lab findings will be pivotal — clean coronary arteries with thrombus suggests APS, intramural hematoma suggests SCAD, and atherosclerotic plaque suggests premature CAD/FH.
