# Round 1: Attending Physician (Internal Medicine)

## Problem Representation

A **38-year-old woman** with a history of **prior myocardial infarction** (highly unusual at this age) presents with **sudden-onset substernal chest pain and dyspnea** occurring during exertion (walking home from work as a mail carrier). She self-administered **aspirin 325 mg and 4 sublingual nitroglycerin tablets without any relief**. EMS was activated.

**Illness script condensed:** Young woman with known premature coronary disease presenting with acute chest pain syndrome refractory to nitroglycerin — concerning for evolving ACS, recurrent coronary dissection, or a non-ischemic emergency.

---

## Key Pivotal Points

1. **Age-sex mismatch for MI:** A 38-year-old woman with prior MI demands investigation into non-atherosclerotic coronary etiologies (SCAD, vasospasm, thrombophilia, FH, FMD).
2. **Nitroglycerin non-response (x4 doses):** This significantly narrows the differential:
   - Argues against pure vasospasm (Prinzmetal angina typically responds to nitrates)
   - Suggests fixed mechanical obstruction (thrombus, dissection flap, severe stenosis) or a non-ischemic cause (dissection, PE, pericarditis)
   - Compatible with evolving STEMI where the vessel is fully occluded
3. **Exertional onset in a physically active person:** Walking is her baseline activity — onset during routine exertion raises concern for a threshold event (plaque rupture, dissection propagation, or demand ischemia on fixed disease).
4. **Self-administration of aspirin + NTG:** She carries these medications, indicating she is aware of her cardiac risk, has been counseled post-MI, and likely has an established cardiac history.
5. **Prior MI at age 38:** This is the single most important data point. It reframes the entire case from "chest pain in a young woman" to "recurrent coronary event in a patient with known premature coronary disease."

---

## Why a 38-Year-Old Woman Has Prior MI (Critical Question)

This is the pivotal framing question of the case. Standard atherosclerotic risk factors rarely produce MI at age 38 in women. The differential for premature MI in young women includes:

| Etiology | Mechanism | Likelihood in This Patient | Key Distinguishing Features |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Spontaneous coronary artery dissection (SCAD)** | Intimal tear or intramural hematoma → vessel occlusion | **High** — #1 cause of MI in women <50; recurrence rate 10-30% | Often peripartum, emotional/physical stress; FMD association in 50-80% |
| **Familial hypercholesterolemia (FH)** | Accelerated atherosclerosis from birth | **Moderate** — heterozygous FH affects 1:250; causes MI in 30s-40s | Tendon xanthomas, corneal arcus, family history of early CAD |
| **Coronary vasospasm (Prinzmetal)** | Focal coronary spasm → transient occlusion | **Lower now** — typically responds to nitrates; her NTG non-response argues against | Rest angina, transient ST elevation, resolves with calcium channel blockers/nitrates |
| **Antiphospholipid syndrome (APS)** | Arterial thrombosis in coronary arteries | **Moderate** — should be considered in any young woman with unexplained arterial event | History of DVT/PE, recurrent pregnancy loss, livedo reticularis |
| **Fibromuscular dysplasia (FMD)** | Non-atherosclerotic arterial wall disease; strongly associated with SCAD | **Moderate** — often co-diagnosed with SCAD | Renal artery stenosis, carotid dissection, "string of beads" on angiography |
| **Cocaine/stimulant use** | Coronary vasospasm + thrombosis + accelerated atherosclerosis | **Must ask** — always screen in young MI patients | Acute hypertension, tachycardia, dilated pupils; urine drug screen |
| **Takotsubo cardiomyopathy** | Catecholamine-mediated apical ballooning | **Lower** — typically older postmenopausal women; prior event was labeled MI | Emotional or physical stress trigger, apical akinesis, normal coronaries |
| **Premature atherosclerosis** | Diabetes, smoking, lupus/RA, HIV, CKD → accelerated plaque | **Possible** — need risk factor inventory | Standard risk factors present at young age; autoimmune disease history |

**Most probable underlying etiology: SCAD**, given the epidemiologic dominance (accounts for ~35% of MI in women <50), the physically active occupation, and the recurrent presentation pattern. SCAD recurrence is a major clinical concern (10-30% over 5 years), and this presentation may represent a recurrent dissection event.

## Clinical Reasoning

### Framing the Problem

This case hinges on two linked questions: (1) What is happening **now**? and (2) What happened **before** (the prior MI)?

The acute presentation — sudden substernal chest pain with dyspnea, refractory to 4 nitroglycerin doses — is most consistent with an **acute coronary syndrome with complete or near-complete vessel occlusion**, or a **non-ischemic cardiovascular emergency** (aortic dissection, massive PE).

### Nitroglycerin Non-Response: What It Tells Us

Nitroglycerin relieves ischemic chest pain by venodilation (reducing preload) and coronary vasodilation. Failure to respond after 4 doses suggests:

- **Complete coronary occlusion** (STEMI) — nitrates cannot dilate past a total occlusion
- **Mechanical obstruction** (dissection flap, large thrombus) — fixed obstruction unresponsive to vasodilators
- **Non-ischemic etiology** — aortic dissection, PE, pericarditis, pneumothorax — none respond to NTG
- **NOT typical vasospasm** — Prinzmetal angina is exquisitely responsive to nitrates, making this diagnosis much less likely

### SCAD Recurrence: The Central Concern

If her prior MI was due to SCAD (the most common cause of MI in women under 50), the recurrence risk is substantial:
- **10-30% recurrence** over follow-up periods of 3-5 years
- Recurrent SCAD often affects a **different coronary artery** than the index event
- Physical exertion is a recognized trigger for SCAD events
- Associated conditions (FMD, present in 50-80% of SCAD patients) increase vascular fragility

### Applying Likelihood Ratios

From the Ch9 Chest Pain reference data:
- **ECG** will be the most immediately informative test: new ST elevation >1mm carries LR+ 5.7-53, while any ECG abnormality has LR- 0.04 (a normal ECG virtually rules out STEMI)
- **Troponin** (sensitivity 95%, specificity 98%, LR+ 48, LR- 0.05) will confirm or exclude myocardial injury
- **Serial CK-MB at 24h** (sensitivity 99%, specificity 98%, LR+ 50, LR- 0.01) provides the highest sensitivity for MI if initial troponin is equivocal
- If aortic dissection is considered: **any feature of the classic triad** (acute pain + pulse deficit + mediastinal widening) has sensitivity 96% and LR- 0.08 — absence of all three features substantially lowers dissection probability

## Recommended Next Steps

### Immediate (in ambulance / on arrival to ED)

1. **12-lead ECG** — the single most important immediate test; look for ST elevation, ST depression, new LBBB
2. **Vital signs** — blood pressure in both arms (>20 mmHg difference raises dissection concern), heart rate, oxygen saturation
3. **Focused physical exam** — pulse deficits, new murmur (aortic regurgitation in dissection), JVD, lung exam for rales or absent breath sounds
4. **IV access, continuous telemetry, supplemental O2 if SpO2 <94%**

### In the ED

5. **High-sensitivity troponin** — stat, with serial measurement at 1-3 hours
6. **Chest X-ray** — mediastinal widening (dissection), pulmonary edema (heart failure), pneumothorax
7. **Basic labs** — CBC, BMP, coagulation studies, D-dimer (if PE or dissection considered)
8. **Review prior cardiac records** — What was the etiology of the prior MI? Was coronary angiography performed? Was SCAD diagnosed? What arteries were involved?
9. **Urine drug screen** — mandatory in all young patients with ACS
10. **Echocardiography** — assess wall motion abnormalities, pericardial effusion, RV strain (PE), aortic root dilation

### If STEMI confirmed on ECG

11. **Activate the cardiac catheterization lab** — emergent coronary angiography with awareness that SCAD may be the underlying etiology (conservative management preferred for SCAD when possible, as PCI can propagate the dissection)
