The cath findings are **pathognomonic**: diffuse coronary artery aneurysms involving **all three coronary territories** (LAD, LCx, RCA) in a young woman. This is NOT:

## Why Kawasaki Disease Is the Leading Diagnosis

### The Classic Late KD Story
1. **Childhood KD** (likely age 1-5, often undiagnosed or forgotten by adulthood)
2. **Coronary aneurysm formation** during acute phase (25% of untreated children)
3. **Giant aneurysms persist** into adulthood and develop:
   - Intimal proliferation and stenosis
   - **Thrombosis within aneurysmal segments** (exactly this case)
   - Calcification
4. **First MI at young age** → stented (the prior inferior MI)
5. **Recurrent thrombotic MI** in a different aneurysmal segment (current anterior STEMI)

### Cath Findings Are Textbook Late KD
- **Aneurysmal + stenotic** segments in series = pathognomonic for post-KD coronary disease
- **Previous stent in LAD** = known coronary disease managed with PCI
- **Thrombosis at tortuous aneurysmal bend** = sluggish flow in aneurysmal sac → thrombus formation
- **All three vessels aneurysmal** = widespread childhood coronary inflammation

### The Microcytic Anemia Connection
- Patients with KD coronary disease are often on **chronic anticoagulation or dual antiplatelet therapy**
- Chronic aspirin/anticoagulation → GI microbleeding → iron deficiency anemia
- Hgb 9.0, MCV 65, RDW 18% = iron deficiency, possibly from chronic antithrombotic therapy
- Alternatively, menorrhagia in a premenopausal woman on antiplatelet agents

### The Prior STENT
- The previously stented LAD segment confirms this patient has **known coronary artery disease**
- The stent was likely placed for a prior KD-related coronary event
- The current occlusion is **just proximal to the stent** — a new thrombotic event in the aneurysmal segment upstream

#### Why Sarcoidosis Deserves Serious Consideration
1. **Papular cutaneous sarcoidosis** — the most common skin manifestation: firm, flesh-colored papules scattered on trunk and extremities
2. **Cardiac sarcoidosis** can cause coronary aneurysms (rare but documented), cardiomyopathy, conduction abnormalities
3. **Normal ESR/CRP** — possible in chronic/burned-out sarcoidosis where granulomas are no longer actively inflaming
4. **ANA 1:320** — can be positive in sarcoidosis (nonspecific)
5. **The cardiomyopathy (EF 25-30%)** — could be partly from sarcoid myocardial infiltration, not just ischemic
6. **Iron deficiency** — GI sarcoidosis could contribute, though menorrhagia/DAPT more likely
7. **Family history** — sarcoidosis has familial clustering

1. **Multiple papules** could be neurofibromas
2. **Hyperpigmented patch** could be a café-au-lait spot (diagnosis requires ≥6 spots >15mm)

### NF1 Vasculopathy — Key Facts
- Occurs in **2-6% of NF1 patients** (likely underrecognized)
- Most commonly affects **renal arteries** (causing renovascular HTN) and **cerebral arteries**
- **Coronary artery involvement is rare but documented** — aneurysms, stenosis, dissection
- Histology: intimal proliferation, medial thinning, adventitial fibrosis
- Driven by loss of neurofibromin → unchecked Ras signaling in vascular smooth muscle
- **Non-inflammatory** — hence normal ESR/CRP (distinguishes from vasculitis)
- Can cause aneurysms AND stenosis in same patient (exactly this case)

## Teaching Points

1. **Kawasaki Disease doesn't end in childhood.** Late coronary sequelae (aneurysms, stenosis, thrombosis, MI) can present decades later. As the first generation of KD survivors reaches middle age, these presentations will increase.

2. **Diffuse coronary artery aneurysms in a young person = KD until proven otherwise.** No other condition produces this pattern as reliably.

3. **The mechanism of MI in KD is thrombosis within aneurysmal segments** — not plaque rupture. Sluggish flow in dilated, tortuous aneurysmal sacs promotes clot formation. This explains why standard antianginal therapy (NTG) didn't work.

4. **Prior stent in a young patient should trigger investigation** into WHY they had coronary disease — the underlying etiology determines surveillance, treatment, and prognosis.

5. **PCI is often technically difficult or impossible** in KD coronary disease due to tortuous aneurysmal anatomy. CABG is frequently the preferred revascularization strategy.

6. **Iron deficiency anemia in patients on chronic antithrombotic therapy** is common and under-recognized. It exacerbates ischemia by reducing oxygen delivery — a vicious cycle in coronary disease.

7. **The prior DDx was wrong — and that's the point.** SCAD (97.5%) and APS (94.7%) seemed highly likely before cath. The lesson: Bayesian reasoning is only as good as the hypotheses considered. KD wasn't in our initial Top 10 because no childhood history was available. Always keep the differential open until definitive data arrives.
